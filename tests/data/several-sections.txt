==Dutch==

===Pronunciation===
* {{IPA|/ɦur/|[ɦuːr]|lang=nl}}
* {{audio|Nl-boer.ogg|audio|lang=nl}}
* {{rhymes|lang=nl|ur}}

===Etymology 1===
{{PIE root|nl|bʰuH}}
From {{inh|nl|dum|bure}}, from {{inh|nl|odt|*būr}}, from {{inh|nl|gem-pro|*būraz||dweller, inhabitant}}, thus originally the same as modern {{m|nl|buur||neighbour}}. The form ''boer'' is that of many eastern dialects including {{cog|li|-}}, where Germanic ''-ū-'' has been retained as a back vowel. In early modern Dutch these two dialectal forms were adopted as semantically distinguished words. Cognate to Old English {{m|ang|būr}}, {{m|ang|ġebūr}} (whence English {{m|en|bower}}) and Old High German {{m|goh|būr}} (whence German {{m|de|Bauer}}).
